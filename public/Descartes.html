More about Descartes

The region surrounding the Descartes crater, known as the Descartes Highlands, was the landing site for the famous Apollo 16 moon landing. Astronauts Commander John Young, Charles Duke, and Ken Mattingly took part in the lunar mission.Young and Duke spent just under three days on the surface, extracting samples from the Descartes Formation and the Cayley Formation.

Descartes was named after the French philosopher, mathematician, and scientist Ren√© Descartes. In terms of philosophy, Descartes is most famous for his concept of dualism. This philosophy revolved around the idea that the mind and body were inherently separate, and must be studied as such. Descartes was a strong proponent of the natural sciences, and advocated for the use of reason to further develop it. Descartes as also notable for his enormous impact on geometry and algebra. Notably, Descartes was the first to use variables in equations (like x, y, and z) to describe unknown quantities, which eventually became a core principle in universal mathematics.
